Manufactured Housing

Affordable
Homes

Developing the right solutions to the affordable housing crisis begins with rethinking how we are developing homes.

Along with empowering resident-owned communities and exploring homebuyer education programs, our support of manufactured housing helps us meet the need for safe and affordable shelter. By allowing fewer dollars to go much further, we are infusing the market with smarter and more attainable options for families who rely on smaller incomes.

Rural Housing

Help where
it's needed

Over two thirds of all counties in the US are considered rural. Creating more opportunities in these markets—which are too often overlooked—allows us to make home a reality for more low- to moderate-income Americans.

By introducing flexible financing solutions like the 3% down Home Possible mortgage and pilot programs like the Sweat Equity and Low-Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC), Freddie Mac is greatly reducing the barrier to homeownership for many hopeful homebuyers in these communities.

Housing Preservation

Keeping it
together

Year over year, renters are spending a greater portion of their income on shelter. In 1960, just 12% of renters spent ½ of their income on rent—today that number has ballooned up to 27%. Without a clear push to drive the industry towards more affordable solutions, it is too easy to imagine a future where this trend continues unchecked.

That’s why Freddie Mac is incentivizing developers to create more stable but modest rental units. Through tax-exempt financing and shared equity programs, we are encouraging borrowers to partner with us in addressing the real needs of communities impacted by the nation’s housing crisis.
